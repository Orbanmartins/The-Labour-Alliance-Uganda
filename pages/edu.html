GENDER / EDUCATION

At The Labour Alliance Uganda, gender issues are given a high priority and strong women representation in all aspects ensures the gender performance is an integral part of Alliance activities. We advocate for equal opportunity for trade and other labour rights for women, THE disability and the youth to work and study in formal, non-formal environment and provide opportunities for women groups to further their quality and that of their staff. .

FEMALE CHILD EDUCATION

The Labour Alliance has set foot as a new breed of national education advocacy partner and we have gained clout. Together with member alliances, we are advocating for new policies in schools management, funding increment, realistic curriculum among a many changes, and we typically support-staffing based on performance measures and the expansion of charter schools, we support-tilt the balance of power away from teachers and unions and towards administrators and funders who, we argue and believe, are less well-versed in the needs of teachers, students, and parents. Our entrance on the education scene as a way of refueling and uniting grass root mobilization because we advocate for respect and support of teachers and education works, increase in Girl Child education, defend academic freedom and protect vulnerable groups. We stand in support of quality public education and we equally support migrant communities in education especially protecting immigrant students, their families from unfair interrogation, detention and deportation. Our campaign for child education stands for “Educate the whole child, don’t teach to test” .
