ABOUT THE LABOUR ALLIANCE UGANDA

The Labour Alliance Uganda represents Employers and Workers forums in which Labour movements in and around Uganda can connect, debate, share knowledge experiences and enforce compliance and strict adherence to labour standards. The Alliance links trade unionists, labour activists, government officials, think-tank, private sector and everyone else interested in descent works into possibilities to network with thousands of trade unionists and labour activism from around the world. .

We are effective at creating a shared sense of identity in our coalition memberships by increasing royalty and motivation. We contribute to the sound administration of workers rights. .

VISSION

To be a leading Alliance Labour Organization in Africa in meeting and managing challenges associated with globalization.

MISSION

To increase the intellectual and strategic capacity of workers organizations and establish stronger working relationships between trade unions, employers, employees, the ILO, sports personalities and the scientific community.

  • OUR MAIN MANDATE

    We offer training, management and human resource needs that match international labor standards. We strengthen trade union capacity and competence to advocate for social justice and descent work at the work place nationally and internationally. What we do at The Labour Alliance Uganda is to build a network of both national and international trade unionists, connect to the global labour movement and have a better understanding of politics and equip workers with knowledge to be able to challenge the dominant neoliberal ideology, skills and networks to be able to organize locally for a fairer society. We carry out collective bargaining, trade union administration, Grievance handling, Leadership and Communications, Labour Management relations, Co-operatives advisory, Training and Management, Trade Unions and the Law., Economics, sociology and Politics, Occupational Health and Safety (including environment), Trade unions and National Development, International Affairs for Trade unionists, Gender issues and family welfare, Productivity bargaining, Job evaluation and Profit sharing schemes, Globalization and the Trade union Movements..

  • FEMALE CHILD EDUCATION

    The Labour Alliance has set foot as a new breed of national education advocacy partner and we have gained clout. Together with member alliances, we are advocating for new policies in schools management, funding increment, realistic curriculum among a many changes, and we typically support-staffing based on performance measures and the expansion of charter schools, we support-tilt the balance of power away from teachers and unions and towards administrators and funders who, we argue and believe, are less well-versed in the needs of teachers, students, and parents.

Our entrance on the education scene as a way of refueling and uniting grass root mobilization because we advocate for respect and support of teachers and education works, increase in Girl Child education, defend academic freedom and protect vulnerable groups. We stand in support of quality public education and we equally support migrant communities in education especially protecting immigrant students, their families from unfair interrogation, detention and deportation. Our campaign for child education stands for Educate the whole child, do not teach to test.
